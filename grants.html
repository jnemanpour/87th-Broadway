Federal Programs

Federal Opportunity Zone (QOF) Tax Benefits

IRS / Treasury Department
Estimated value: $3–8M+ in tax savings
What: Invest capital gains into a Qualified Opportunity Fund (QOF) targeting this census tract. OZ program made permanent by OBBBA (July 2025).
Benefits: 5-year deferral of capital gains + 10% basis step-up at 5 yrs + 15% at 7 yrs + permanent exclusion of gains on QOF investment held 10+ years. No depreciation recapture.
Eligibility: Census Tract 6037292000 confirmed OZ. OZ 1.0 sunsets end of 2026; OZ 2.0 begins Jan 1, 2027.
Action: Engage QOF attorney. Structure acquisition through a QOF entity.

Federal Low-Income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC)

CA Tax Credit Allocation Committee (CTCAC)
Estimated equity: $8–22M
What: 4% and 9% tax credits for affordable housing. Major expansion under OBBBA: 9% allocations permanently increased 12%; 4% bond threshold reduced from 50% to 25%.
4% Credits: Paired with tax-exempt bonds. Easier to obtain. Round 2 deadline: May 19, 2026.
9% Credits: Highly competitive. Round 1 deadline: April 7, 2026. Higher per-unit equity.
Relevance: Adjacent project (8727 S Broadway, 122 units) used LIHTC equity. This is the primary funding mechanism for affordable development on this corridor.
Next deadlines: 9% Round 1 — April 7, 2026 | 4% Round 2 — May 19, 2026

Federal New Markets Tax Credits (NMTC)

CDFI Fund / Treasury
Estimated subsidy: $3–8M
What: 39% tax credit over 7 years on qualified equity investments in low-income census tracts. Program made permanent at $5B/year under OBBBA.
Eligibility: South LA qualifies. Can be used for commercial and mixed-use components.
Action: Apply through a certified Community Development Entity (CDE). CY2026 round expected mid-2026.

Federal Historic Tax Credits

National Park Service / IRS
Estimated: $1–2M
What: 20% federal credit on qualified rehabilitation costs for certified historic structures.
Eligibility: The 1910 store (8613 S Broadway) and 1927 restaurant (8623 S Broadway) may qualify if on or eligible for the National Register of Historic Places.
Action: Commission a historic assessment for both buildings. Can stack with LIHTC.

State SB 35 / SB 423 — Streamlined Ministerial Approval

CA Dept. of Housing & Community Development
Value: Eliminates CEQA, speeds approval 6-12 months
What: By-right ministerial approval for housing on infill sites in jurisdictions not meeting housing goals. No CEQA, no public hearing, zero parking near transit.
Eligibility: C2 zoning, infill site, LA is not meeting RHNA targets. Must include affordable units (50% for 10+ unit projects).
Action: Confirm eligibility via ZIMAS. Design with 50%+ affordable units to qualify.

State AB 2011 — Affordable Housing on Commercial Land

CA State Legislature
Value: By-right housing on C2 land, no rezoning needed
What: Allows by-right residential development on commercially-zoned land. Ministerial approval, no CEQA.
Eligibility: C2 zoning qualifies. Must pay prevailing wages and include affordable units.
Relevance: Eliminates the need for a zone change or CUP to build housing on these C2 parcels.

City of Los Angeles

City CHIP / MIIP Density Bonus

LA Dept. of City Planning
Up to 120% density bonus + 55% FAR increase
What: Community Housing Incentive Program / Mixed Income Incentive Program, adopted February 2025. Most powerful density bonus available in LA.
Benefits: 120% density bonus, 55% FAR increase, ministerial review, exempt from Linkage Fee, reduced setbacks, height bonuses.
Eligibility: C2 zone qualifies. Must include affordable units at specified AMI levels.
Impact: On 90,000 SF of C2 land, this could enable 300-500+ units depending on height district.
