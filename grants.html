Federal Opportunity Zone (QOF) Tax Benefits ▼

IRS / Treasury Department
Estimated value: $3–8M+ in tax savings
What: Invest capital gains into a Qualified Opportunity Fund (QOF) targeting this census tract. OZ program made permanent by OBBBA (July 2025).
Benefits: 5-year deferral of capital gains + 10% basis step-up at 5 yrs + 15% at 7 yrs + permanent exclusion of gains on QOF investment held 10+ years. No depreciation recapture.
Eligibility: Census Tract 6037292000 confirmed OZ. OZ 1.0 sunsets end of 2026; OZ 2.0 begins Jan 1, 2027.
Action: Engage QOF attorney. Structure acquisition through a QOF entity.

How to Get It — Step by Step

  1. Form a Qualified Opportunity Fund (QOF): Create an LLC or LP and self-certify as a QOF by filing IRS Form 8996 with your tax return. The entity must hold ≥90% of assets in qualifying OZ property.
  2. Engage an OZ-specialized attorney: Structure the $5M acquisition through the QOF entity from day one. The capital gains must be invested within 180 days of the triggering sale event.
  3. Invest capital gains into the QOF: You (or outside investors with capital gains) contribute gains into the fund. Only the gain amount qualifies — not basis.
  4. Deploy capital into OZ property: The QOF must substantially improve the property — spend more than basis in improvements within 30 months. For new construction, the substantial improvement test is automatically met.
  5. Hold for 10+ years: After 10 years, elect to step up basis on the QOF investment to fair market value — permanently excluding all appreciation from tax.
  6. File annually: Submit IRS Form 8996 each year to certify the 90% asset test. File Form 8949 in the year of original gain to elect deferral.

  6. Close the NMTC transaction: Complex leverage structure — typically yields ~20-25% of the Qualified Equity Investment as net benefit to the project after fees.
  7. 7-year compliance period: Maintain qualified use for 7 years. After year 7, leverage loan is forgiven = permanent subsidy.
