## 1.1 OPPORTUNITY ZONES (Qualified Opportunity Fund)

### Status: CONFIRMED ELIGIBLE -- CENSUS TRACT 6037292000

**Administering Agency:** IRS / U.S. Treasury
**Relevance to Project:** VERY HIGH -- The 87th & Broadway site is in a designated Opportunity Zone.

### Current OZ 1.0 (Through December 31, 2026)
- Census Tract 6037292000 covering South Broadway / 90003 is a **confirmed designated Opportunity Zone**
- Current OZ designations sunset at end of 2026 (accelerated from 2028 by the One Big Beautiful Bill Act)
- Capital gains invested before December 31, 2026 qualify under OZ 1.0 rules
- **Mandatory recognition event:** Deferred gains must be recognized on December 31, 2026

### OZ 2.0 (Starting January 1, 2027) -- MADE PERMANENT by OBBBA
The One Big Beautiful Bill Act (signed July 4, 2025) makes the OZ program **permanent**:

| Feature | OZ 1.0 (through 2026) | OZ 2.0 (2027+) |
|---------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Gain Deferral | Until Dec 31, 2026 | 5 years from investment date |
| Basis Step-Up (5 yr) | 10% | 10% |
| Basis Step-Up (7 yr) | 15% (expired for new investments) | Eliminated |
| 10-Year Gain Exclusion | Yes | Yes, plus NO depreciation recapture |
| Duration Cap | None | 30-year FMV cap |
| Zone Redesignation | 2018 map | Governors redesignate every 10 years |

### Redesignation Timeline (Critical for This Project)
- Governors must redesignate QOZs within 90-day period beginning July 1, 2026
- Treasury approves within 60 days
- New OZ 2.0 map effective January 1, 2027
- South LA / 90003 has **extremely strong case** for redesignation given top-5% disadvantaged status

### Tax Planning Strategy for 87th & Broadway
**Option A -- Invest in 2026 under OZ 1.0:**
- Defer 2026 capital gains into QOF
- Mandatory recognition of prior deferred gains on Dec 31, 2026
- 10-year exclusion available if held through 2036

**Option B -- Wait for OZ 2.0 (PREFERRED for gains realized mid-2026 or later):**
- If gain realized on/after August 1, 2026, the 180-day reinvestment window extends into 2027
- OZ 2.0 provides 5-year deferral (not forced recognition at year-end)
- More favorable long-term treatment

### Estimated Value
For $4.8M acquisition + $15-20M development:
- Capital gains deferral on invested gains
- Permanent exclusion of ALL appreciation if held 10+ years
- No depreciation recapture under OZ 2.0
- **Potential tax savings: $3-8M+ depending on appreciation and capital gains invested**

2. Engage OZ-specialized attorney to structure Qualified Opportunity Fund
3. Determine timing strategy (OZ 1.0 vs. OZ 2.0) based on gain realization dates
4. File Form 8997 annually with federal tax return

## 1.5 FEDERAL & STATE HISTORIC TAX CREDITS

### Status: POTENTIALLY APPLICABLE FOR 1910/1927 BUILDINGS

**Administering Agency:** National Park Service / IRS / California OHP
**Relevance to Project:** MODERATE-HIGH if buildings are historically significant

### Federal Historic Tax Credit (HTC)
- **20% credit** on qualified rehabilitation expenditures for certified historic structures
- **10% credit** for rehabilitation of non-historic, non-residential buildings built before 1936
- Buildings from 1910 and 1927 qualify under both potential pathways
- Only available for income-producing properties (commercial, rental residential)
- **NOTE:** OBBBA did NOT include HTC provisions -- program continues under existing rules

### Requirements
1. **National Register listing** (individual or as contributing structure in a historic district)
2. **Substantial Rehabilitation Test:** Rehab costs must exceed pre-rehabilitation building value within 24 months (or 60 months for phased projects)
3. **Secretary of the Interior's Standards:** 10 principles ensuring historic character preservation
4. **Income-producing use** required (no owner-occupied residential)

### California State Historic Tax Credit
- **Additional state credit** -- up to $50M annually in state tax credits statewide
- Can be combined with federal 20% credit for potentially **40%+ total credit**
- Administered by California Office of Historic Preservation (OHP)

### Mills Act (City of LA)
- Property tax reduction for Historic-Cultural Monuments (HCMs) and HPOZ contributing structures
- Potential 50-70% property tax reduction
- Application through LA City Planning

### California Historical Building Code (CHBC)
- Owners of qualified historic properties can use CHBC for rehabilitation
- More flexible than current code requirements for new construction
- Reduces rehab costs significantly

### Action Required for 87th & Broadway
1. Research whether 1910/1927 buildings are listed or eligible for National Register
2. Commission a historic resources survey
3. If eligible, engage OHP for preliminary consultation
4. Structure rehabilitation to meet Secretary's Standards
5. Apply for Mills Act if buildings receive HCM designation

### Estimated Value
If $5M rehabilitation qualifies:
- Federal 20% HTC = **$1,000,000** in tax credits
- State HTC (additional) = potential **$500,000-1,000,000**
- Mills Act = ongoing property tax savings of **$50,000-100,000/year**

## 2.7 STATE DENSITY BONUS LAW (AB 2345 + AB 1287)

### Status: GUARANTEED ENTITLEMENT -- UP TO 50%+ BONUS

**Administering Agency:** Local jurisdiction (applies statewide by right)
**Relevance to Project:** HIGH -- baseline density bonus for any project with affordable units

### Current Law (2026)
- Maximum density bonus: **50%** (increased from 35% by AB 2345, effective 2021)
- To achieve 50% bonus, project must set aside:
  - 15% of units for Very Low Income, OR
  - 24% of units for Low Income, OR
  - 44% of for-sale units for Moderate Income

### Incentives/Concessions
- 1-3 additional incentives based on affordable unit percentage
- Includes: yard reductions, open space relief, lot coverage deviations
- Reduced parking: 1 space per studio/1BR, 1.5 spaces per 2-3BR
- No guest or covered parking required

### AB 1287 "Stacked" Bonuses (2023)
- Projects exceeding minimum affordability can qualify for **even higher density limits**
- Bonuses can stack beyond the 50% maximum in certain cases

### Key Principle
- Local government **cannot apply any development standard** that would physically preclude a project using its entitled density bonus and incentives
- Ministerial review for most density bonus projects

### Comparison to MIIP
The State Density Bonus Law provides a **floor** of entitlements. The CHIP/MIIP program (Section 3.1) provides **additional local bonuses** that can exceed state law. Developer should pursue whichever provides the greatest benefit.

## Comprehensive Density Bonus Comparison for 87th & Broadway

### Available Pathways (Developer Chooses Best Option)

| Program | Max Density Bonus | Max FAR Increase | Parking Relief | Approval Type | Affordability Requirement |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|----------------|---------------|--------------------------|
| **State DBL (AB 2345)** | 50% | N/A (state law) | Reduced | Ministerial | 15% VLI / 24% LI / 44% Mod |
| **CHIP/MIIP (TOC Area)** | **120%** | **55%** | Significant | Ministerial | Per MIIP tier (99-yr covenant) |
| **SB 35/423** | Per zone | Per zone | **Zero** (near transit) | Ministerial (no CEQA) | Per city housing progress |
| **AB 2011** | Per zone | Per zone | Reduced | Ministerial (no CEQA) | Per program requirements |
| **ED1** | Per zone | Per zone | Reduced | Ministerial | 100% at/below 80% AMI |
| **Adaptive Reuse (ARO)** | Existing floor area | Existing height retained | Existing parking | By-right permit | None for conversion |

### RECOMMENDED PATHWAY: CHIP/MIIP
- **120% density bonus + 55% FAR increase** is the most generous available
- Ministerial review = no discretionary hearings
- Exempt from Affordable Linkage Fee
- 4 additional on-menu or off-menu incentives
- Combined with SB 35/423 for CEQA exemption and zero parking

### C2 Zone Base Standards (Must Verify on ZIMAS)
- C2 zone allows residential uses (multifamily by right)
- Base density and FAR depend on **height district suffix** (e.g., C2-1VL, C2-2, etc.)
- Nearby 8801 S Broadway is C2-1VL-CPIO (Community Plan Implementation Overlay)
- C2-1VL typically allows R4-equivalent residential density

### Estimated Development Potential
Assuming C2-1VL with R4-equivalent density on 90,000 SF site:
- **Base density:** ~1 unit per 400 SF = ~225 units
- **With 120% MIIP bonus:** ~495 units
- **With 55% FAR increase:** substantial additional building envelope
- **Actual yield** depends on unit sizes, common areas, parking, setbacks

**NOTE:** These are rough estimates. Exact calculations require ZIMAS verification of height district, community plan designation, and MIIP tier. Engage a land use consultant for precise entitlement analysis.
