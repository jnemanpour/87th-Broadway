# 3. SBA 504 LOANS

## Status: APPLICABLE FOR ACQUISITION & DEVELOPMENT

### Program Structure
- **Bank/lender: 50%** (first lien)
- **CDC/SBA: 40%** (second lien, up to $5.5M)
- **Borrower: 10% down** (15% for startups, 20% for startup + special purpose)

### Key Terms
- Maximum SBA portion: $5.5M
- 20-year fixed rate for real estate
- Below-market interest rates
- No limit on overall project size

### Eligibility Requirements (Updated 2026)
- Tangible net worth < $20M
- Average net income < $6.5M (after taxes, prior 2 years)
- Must occupy 51%+ of existing building (61% for new construction)
- All 20%+ owners must be U.S. citizens or LPRs
- SBSS credit score minimum: 165 (increased from 155 in April 2025)

### Application for This Project
For $4.8M Vernon acquisition:
- Bank provides ~$2.4M (50%)
- SBA/CDC provides ~$1.92M (40%)
- Borrower provides ~$480K (10%)
- Fixed 20-year rate, current rates per monthly SBA funding cycle

### CDCs Operating in LA Area
- SCED (Southern California Enterprise Development)
- Various CDCs licensed for California -- use SBA CDC locator

### Environmental Note
SBA has changed environmental review process for 504 loans -- if no contamination identified, lender certifies and maintains report in file.

# 4. HUD SECTION 108 / CDBG

## Status: AVAILABLE THROUGH LOCAL GOVERNMENT APPLICATION

### Program Overview
- Communities can borrow up to 5x their annual CDBG allocation
- Low-cost, flexible financing for economic development
- Non-competitive -- applications accepted on rolling basis
- Can be passed through to for-profit developers

### Eligible Uses
- Real property acquisition
- Rehabilitation of publicly owned property
- Public facilities construction
- Economic development activities
- Infrastructure to support development

### Requirements
- Must meet CDBG national objective (usually benefit to low/moderate income persons)
- At least 51% of jobs must be held by or available to LMI persons
- Local government must pledge current and future CDBG allocations as security
- Additional collateral required (property liens, tax increment, etc.)

### 2025 Housing Focus
- New HUD initiative: $250M of $400M loan guarantee authority for housing
- Includes adaptive reuse of commercial/industrial to housing
- Application deadline: July 1, 2025

### How to Access
- Contact LA County Community Development Commission (LACDC)
- City of Vernon could apply as CDBG recipient
- For 8701 Broadway: City of Los Angeles CDBG administrator

# 11. DTSC VOLUNTARY CLEANUP / BROWNFIELDS

## Status: ESSENTIAL FOR INDUSTRIAL PROPERTY DUE DILIGENCE

### Voluntary Cleanup Program (VCP)
- Developer enters agreement with DTSC
- Pays for site investigation and cleanup costs
- Reimburses DTSC for oversight staff time
- Expedites agency review and approval

### Bona Fide Purchaser Protections
- Limited liability protection for qualifying buyers
- Must complete All Appropriate Inquiries (AAI) within 1 year before purchase
- Phase I ESA must follow full ASTM protocol
- Several elements expire after 180 days; entire report must be <1 year old at closing

### Prospective Purchaser Agreements (PPAs)
- Individually negotiated with DTSC legal counsel
- Releases purchaser from environmental liability upon cleanup completion
- DTSC covenants not to sue for pre-existing contamination
- Must meet remedial actions and cost contribution conditions

### CLRRA (California Land Reuse and Revitalization Act)
- Provides immunity and liability protections for:
  - Bona Fide Purchasers
  - Innocent Land Owners
  - Contiguous Property Owners
- Must demonstrate AAI conducted within 1 year before purchase

### AB 440 Protections
- Local municipal authorities can promote remediation
- Parties completing cleanups under AB 440 receive immunity from liability

### Strategy for Vernon Parcels
1. **Immediately** commission Phase I ESA for all 15 parcels
2. If contamination found, enter DTSC VCP
3. Negotiate Prospective Purchaser Agreement
4. Apply for EPA Brownfields Assessment/Cleanup grants through City of Vernon
5. Secure Bona Fide Purchaser status through proper AAI documentation

# 16. HISTORIC PRESERVATION / ADAPTIVE REUSE

## Status: POTENTIALLY APPLICABLE FOR OLDER INDUSTRIAL BUILDINGS

### Federal Historic Tax Credit (HTC)
- 20% credit on qualified rehabilitation expenditures
- Building must be listed on National Register of Historic Places
- Must be income-producing (commercial, industrial, rental)
- Credit allocated over 5 years
- Can be combined with state credits for 40%+ of capital stack

### California SB 451 Historic Tax Credit
- 20% state credit (25% for certain projects including affordable housing, designated census tracts)
- Annual cap: $50M statewide ($10M set aside for residential/smaller projects)
- **SUNSET: January 1, 2026** -- program in final year
- Legislative renewal uncertain

### Mills Act (Property Tax Reduction)
- Local governments can enter contracts with historic property owners
- Significant property tax reduction (often 40-60%)
- Must be individually listed historic resource
- 10-year renewable contracts

### Application to This Project
- Vernon industrial buildings may have historic significance if built pre-1970s
- Conduct historic resource evaluation of existing structures
- If any buildings qualify for National Register listing:
  - Federal 20% + State 20-25% = 40-45% credit on rehabilitation costs
  - On $5M rehabilitation: $2M-$2.25M in credits

# 19. STACKING STRATEGY & CAPITAL STACK

## RECOMMENDED CAPITAL STACK MODEL

### Hypothetical Project: $20M Total Development Cost
(Acquisition $4.8M + Improvements $15.2M)

| Source | % of Stack | Amount | Type |
|--------|-----------|--------|------|
| SBA 504 (Bank portion) | 24% | $4.8M | Senior debt |
| SBA 504 (CDC portion) | 10% | $1.92M | Subordinate debt |
| NMTC subsidy | 15-20% | $3-4M | Equity-like subsidy |
| C-PACE | 15-20% | $3-4M | Property assessment |
| Opportunity Zone equity | 10-15% | $2-3M | QOF investment |
| IRA/Solar tax credits | 3-5% | $600K-$1M | Tax credit equity |
| Cal Competes credit | 2-5% | $400K-$1M | State tax credit |
| EPA/State grants | 2-3% | $400K-$600K | Grant (if brownfield) |
| Vernon utility incentives | 1% | $100K-$200K | Rebates/savings |
| **Owner equity** | **5-10%** | **$1M-$2M** | Cash |

### Key Stacking Principles
1. **NMTC + OZ**: Can be combined but requires careful structuring (different compliance periods)
2. **SBA 504 + NMTC**: SBA loan can be part of NMTC leverage structure
3. **C-PACE + conventional debt**: C-PACE is non-recourse, secured by property tax assessment, does not conflict with other debt
4. **Tax credits stack**: Federal ITC + State credits + WOTC + NEC can all be claimed simultaneously
5. **Grants layer on top**: EPA brownfields, EDA, and state grants reduce basis but don't conflict with tax credit programs

### Critical Coordination Requirements
- OZ compliance period (10 years for max benefit)
- NMTC compliance period (7 years)
- SBA 504 occupancy requirements (51%/61%)
- C-PACE lender consent from senior lender
- Environmental cleanup timeline alignment with development schedule
- Tax credit investor requirements and exit strategies
